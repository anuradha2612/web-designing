Goshala History

Cow and its family, including Ox and Bull, has been playing an important role in shaping our country culture and its development traditionally. It has been a part and parcel of our culture supporting and maintaining the same all through the ages since hundreds and thousands of years. It has been the backbone and moving the wheels of the agricultural culture of our country traditionally. This animal specie has been one of the major contributor in supporting the eco-cycle of the nature. The dung it produces has been enriching the fertility of our soil, since from ages, which in turn has been enhancing and promoting our agriculture. Again we see that the cowdung has been one of the major domestic fuel in rural India, which comprises our country 75% of the population. We should not forget that it is these Oxen that have been ploughing our agricultural fields since ages and supported our agricultural movement. They have also been running our Bullock carts, thus saving petroleum fuel and keeping our environment pollution free. Cow milk is considered as the healtiest among the milch animals and edible for all ages of humanbeings. It is considered the second best after the mother’s milk for the new born human kind. The Goumutra(Cow Urine) is used in preparing medicines for various ailments like BP, Chlolestrol, diabetis etc. including cancer. Though automation has crept in Indian agricultural scenario, we are still largely dependent upon Cow family in running our agricultural show in our country. Since it is found that atleast 60% of the farmers are of small scale, who do not require automation machineries.

Hence, we see that we have innumerable advantages of Cow family in sustaining this animal, which no any other animal specie has given to mankind all under one roof. Therefore one should not wish to terminate its life for its flesh and leather, to serve the dinner/lunch for a section of the society. Even if a cow is aged and stops providing milk, mind it, it can give us cowdung till it faces its natural death, thus supporting our agriculture upon which we rely our basic living of our food grain supply. Basically an agricultural country like India should never opt for Cow slaughter. A pound of ‘Beef’ otherwise can be worth feeding many with milk and food grains. Ban on Cow slaughter is not against any religion, caste/creed or it is not that cow favours a particular section of a society. It is a messiah for the mankind. Even Indian Muslim Kings such as Babur, Humayun, Akbar or even the most unpopular King Aurangzeb including Haider Ali Khan and Tippu Sultan, had abolished Cow slaughter in their Kingdoms.
